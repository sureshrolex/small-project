Bitter gourd

Bitter gourd, also known as bitter melon or Momordica charantia, is a unique vegetable widely cultivated and consumed in many parts of the world. Despite its somewhat bitter taste, bitter gourd is cherished for its numerous health benefits and culinary versatility. This vegetable, characterized by its oblong shape and bumpy exterior, belongs to the gourd family

Carrot

Originally cultivated in Central Asia, carrots have been cultivated for thousands of years and are now grown in various colors, including orange, purple, red, white, and yellow. The most common variety is the orange carrot, which is rich in beta-carotene, a precursor to vitamin A. Carrots are packed with essential nutrients, including vitamins A, C, K, and B6, as well as potassium, biotin, and fiber.

Drum stick

Drumsticks, scientifically known as Moringa oleifera, are slender, elongated pods derived from the drumstick tree, a fast-growing, drought-resistant plant native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Africa These pods have a characteristic shape, resembling drumsticks, hence the name. They typically range from 1 to 2 feet in length and are green in color. Inside the pods are small, round seeds that are also edible.

Onion

Onions are bulbous vegetables widely used in cooking for their distinct flavor and aroma. They belong to the Allium genus and are closely related to garlic, shallots, leeks, and chives. Onions come in various colors, including white, yellow, and red, each with its own flavor profile and culinary uses. onions are an essential kitchen staple that not only enhances the flavor of dishes but also offers a range of potential health benefits.

Pototo

Potatoes, scientifically known as Solanum tuberosum, are starchy tuberous vegetables that are a staple food in many parts of the world. They are native to the Andes region of South America and have been cultivated for thousands of years. potatoes are a versatile and nutritious vegetable that can be incorporated into a wide range of dishes. Whether baked, boiled, mashed, or fried, potatoes are a delicious and satisfying addition to any meal.

Pumpkin

Pumpkin, scientifically known as Cucurbita pepo, is a type of winter squash that belongs to the gourd family. It's native to North America and is now cultivated in many regions around the world. Pumpkins are known for their large, round shape with smooth, ribbed skin, and vibrant orange color, although they can also come in different shapes, sizes, and colors.

Ridge gourd

Ridge gourd, scientifically known as Luffa acutangula, is a tropical vegetable commonly cultivated in Asia, particularly in India, China, and Southeast Asian countries. It belongs to the gourd family and is closely related to other gourds like sponge gourd and bitter gourd.

Tomoto

Tomato, scientifically known as Solanum lycopersicum, is a widely cultivated fruit that is often used as a vegetable in cooking. It belongs to the nightshade family, along with potatoes, eggplants, and bell peppers. Native to western South America, tomatoes are now grown and consumed globally, playing a central role in many cuisines worldwide
